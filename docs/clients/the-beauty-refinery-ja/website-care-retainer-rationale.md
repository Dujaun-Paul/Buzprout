## Purpose of this document

This explains the **monthly care fee** for The Beauty Refinery JA once the site and tools are live: what the business receives, why it exists, and how it stays fair.

The project is no longer just a brochure website. It is a **working system**: online booking, a staff admin dashboard, application intake, and automated client emails. Care is the cost of keeping that system secure, accurate, and supported while the salon uses it every week.

---

## 1. What is live (and what care is protecting)

What the business runs on day to day:

- **Public website** — brand, services, hours, locations, policies, training info  
- **Online booking** — appointments tied to deposits and clear instructions  
- **Admin dashboard** — staff login, bookings overview, applications, insights, team roles  
- **Application intake** — retreat / training forms stored for the team to review  
- **Email automation** — post-appointment thank-you and monthly rebook reminders (with staff overrides)

Care is not “server rent.” It is **ownership of that whole stack** so bookings, staff tools, and client emails keep working when prices change, browsers update, or something fails before a busy weekend.

---

## 2. Infrastructure is not the same as care

The system runs on layers of technology: **hosting**, **deployment**, **database**, **authentication**, **storage**, **domain**, **DNS**, **SSL**, and **transactional email**.

Those platforms provide the foundation. That is where the site lives, how staff sign in, where bookings and applications are stored, and how emails are sent.

They do **not** provide:

- Updating service prices, hours, or policies on the live site  
- Fixing bugs after a browser or software update  
- Checking that booking, deposits, admin login, and emails still work end to end  
- Responding when the Book button, admin panel, or form stops working  
- Guarding against security issues in dependencies  
- Monitoring that forms, contact links, and automated emails still behave correctly  
- Someone who knows *this* project when something breaks  

**Platform fees are rent for tools. Care is professional time and responsibility.**

Even when hosting, database, or email tiers start at low or no cost, someone must still own them: renew the domain, watch usage limits, plan upgrades before traffic or email volume grows, and step in when something fails.

---

## 3. Nothing is free: there is always a cost

There is no such thing as a live booking and admin system with zero ongoing cost. The only question is whether you pay in **cash**, **time**, or **lost opportunity**.

**If you pay in cash:** hosting, domain renewal, database usage, email sending, backups, and professional care.

**If you pay in time:** you or your staff troubleshoot outages, chase broken booking links, reset staff logins, or wonder why clients never got the rebook email.

**If you pay in opportunity:** stale prices on the site, a broken booking flow over a weekend, applications sitting unseen because the dashboard is down, or customers who give up because the site does not work.

Free or low-cost tiers still have limits. Those limits change. Usage caps appear. Paid plans become necessary as bookings, staff accounts, and emails grow. Domains and DNS still need management every year.

A monthly care fee budgets for that before it becomes an emergency invoice or a quiet loss of bookings no one notices until Monday.

### Email and follow-up (once live)

- Oversight that post-appointment and rebook emails are sending as configured  
- Help adjusting simple templates or send timing when needed  
- Alerting if email volume is approaching plan limits  

### Support and availability

- A clear point of contact for site and admin issues  
- Response within 1 to 2 business days for non-critical issues; priority attention for outages when possible  
- Guidance when a request is a quick tweak vs a new feature  

### Infrastructure awareness

- Oversight of hosting, deployment, database, and email health  
- Alerting when a plan limit may need an upgrade  
- Advice before costs spike unexpectedly

## 5. What is not included

To keep the monthly fee fair and predictable, these are billed separately or handled directly by the client:

- Full redesign or rebrand  
- New major features beyond what is already live (e.g. card payments, SMS, Google Calendar sync, new custom modules)  
- Custom photography or branding packages  
- Paid advertising or SEO campaigns  
- Third-party invoices (domain renewals, hosting upgrades, database plans, email plan upgrades), at cost or paid directly by the client  
- Emergency after-hours work beyond agreed hours, unless separately scoped  
- Large new forms, deep dashboard rebuilds, or multi-week feature work  

This keeps the retainer from becoming unlimited unpaid development. **Caring for** the admin dashboard and emails is included; **building brand-new systems** is not.

## 6. Why JMD $5,000 makes sense

### Running costs (categories, not vendors)

- **Domain:** Yearly renewal; needs management  
- **Hosting / deployment:** Low cost at first; paid tiers as traffic and features grow  
- **Database / auth / storage:** Holds bookings, applications, and staff accounts; paid tiers with usage and backups  
- **Transactional email:** Post-appointment and rebook messages; free tiers have caps; paid tiers as volume grows  
- **DNS / SSL:** Occasional fixes and renewals  

Even when platform costs stay modest, **someone must own them**. That ownership is part of care.

### Professional time (the real value)

At JMD $5,000/month, this is roughly **1 to 3 hours** of specialist care per month — a light retainer, not a large agency contract.

One broken booking weekend, or a week of missed rebook emails, can cost the business more than one month of care in lost deposits and appointments. Care is priced to prevent that, not to match server rent line by line.

### Risk transfer

**Without care:** issues wait until someone is free; every fix becomes a rush quote; prices and policies go stale; admin access problems pile up; email automation drifts or fails quietly.

**With care:** there is a clear owner; small updates are expected; downtime, booking failures, and email issues are treated as priority.

## 7. Why not "just call when something breaks"?

Pay-as-you-go sounds cheaper. It usually costs more and slows everything down:

1. **Slow response:** no reserved capacity  
2. **Higher per-fix rates:** emergency pricing  
3. **No proactive updates:** the site drifts from real prices and hours  
4. **Awkward small jobs:** a 20-minute price update or staff login reset becomes admin overhead  
5. **Silent failures:** emails and forms can break without anyone noticing until clients complain  

**JMD $5,000/month is the simple option:** predictable for the client, sustainable for the team doing the work.
