## How Buzprout prices work

Buzprout is not a template shop. We help Caribbean businesses fix real problems: more bookings, fewer manual messages, better customer experience. We do that using websites, booking flows, and custom tools.

**Our pricing model:**

1. **Identify the business outcome:** what is the problem costing them in time, revenue, or trust?
2. **Match a tier:** Entry (visibility), Growth (conversion), Systems (operations), or Retainers (ongoing improvement)
3. **Quote a firm price:** after a free call, not before

If a client only says "I need a website," the right question is: *What problem should that website solve?* A brochure site and a booking-dependent salon site are not the same investment.

## Nothing is free: there is always a cost

Clients often assume that because hosting or database tiers can start cheap, the whole thing should stay cheap forever. That is not how live business technology works.

Every project and every live site carries cost in one or more forms:

**Cash:** professional design and development, hosting, domain renewal, database usage, backups, and ongoing care.

**Time:** the client or their staff troubleshooting outages, updating prices manually, or chasing broken booking links.

**Opportunity:** stale prices on the site, a broken booking flow over a busy weekend, or customers who leave because the site does not work or look credible.

**Platform rent is not care.** Hosting, deployment, and database services provide infrastructure. They do not update service menus, fix bugs after software updates, or answer when the Book button stops working on a Friday night.

Referrers can help clients understand: **the build fee creates the asset; the care fee keeps it reliable; infrastructure bills are separate and still need someone to own them.**

## Payment options: how clients can pay the build

Not every client can pay the full build upfront. Buzprout offers structured options; the right one depends on tier, cash flow, and eligibility.

### Milestone build (default)

**Structure:** 40% deposit at signing, then staged payments at design approval and launch.  
**Best for:** Clients who can fund the project in three payments.  
**Ownership:** Full ownership when the final milestone clears.  
**Example:** JMD $200,000 project → JMD $80,000 signing, $60,000 design, $60,000 launch.

### Buzprout Flex: build now, pay monthly

**Structure:** Deposit (typically 40%) + monthly installments over 6 to 18 months, depending on tier.  
**Plan fee:** 10% (6 months), 15% (12 months), or 20% (18 months) on the financed portion only.  
**Best for:** Growth and Systems builds where the client needs the site live before paying in full.  
**Ownership:** Transfers when the plan is complete. Early payoff discount available (5%).  
**Example:** JMD $200,000 Business Website + Systems → JMD $80,000 deposit + ~JMD $11,500/month × 12 months.

**Eligibility:** Valid ID, business or revenue proof, signed contract, deposit paid before work starts.

### Build + Care: lower deposit, bundled support

**Structure:** 25% deposit + 18 monthly payments that bundle build cost, hosting, and light care.  
**Best for:** Entry-tier sites only, when even a Flex deposit is a stretch.  
**Not available for:** Systems-tier builds.  
**Example:** JMD $80,000 Starter Website → JMD $20,000 deposit + JMD $5,000/month × 18 months.

## Ongoing care: monthly fees after launch

The build fee ends at launch. **Care continues while the site is in use.**

Care is not "charging for free hosting." It is a predictable fee for **ownership, updates, security, and reliability** especially when bookings or deposits depend on the site.

### Website Care tiers

**Care Essential:** JMD $4,000 to $6,000/month** (~USD $25 to $38/month)  
Hosting oversight, SSL, backups, uptime checks, security patches, email support (48-hour response).  
Edits billed separately. Best for brochure sites that rarely change.

**Care Standard:** JMD $8,000 to $12,000/month** (~USD $50 to $76/month)  
Everything in Essential, plus 2 hours of content or design edits per month, WhatsApp support, monthly health report.  
**Recommended for most active business sites** that need regular price or content updates.

**Care Plus:** JMD $15,000 to $22,000/month** (~USD $95 to $140/month)  
Everything in Standard, plus 5 edit hours, same-day priority response, quarterly analytics review.  
For tourism, campaigns, and teams that need fast turnaround.

### Systems retainers (custom software clients)

**Business Systems Retainer:** JMD $35,000 to $120,000/month**  
Ongoing fixes, features, monitoring, and predictable development time for custom platforms.

**Growth Retainer:** JMD $80,000 to $200,000/month**  
Roadmap features, automation, reports, and a long-term improvement partner.

## Why these prices are fair: justification referrers can use

### Build fees reflect business value, not page count

A booking site for a salon is not priced like a five-page brochure because it affects **revenue operations:** appointments, deposits, policies, two locations, and service menus that change. The build creates a system the business depends on.

### Caribbean market context

Buzprout prices in JMD with USD equivalents. Ranges sit below what large agencies charge for comparable work, while reflecting real specialist time, design, development, testing, launch, and handoff.

### Care is intentionally modest

At JMD $5,000 to $8,000/month, care is roughly **1 to 4 hours** of specialist attention per month. It is not an open-ended support contract. One broken booking weekend can cost a salon more than several months of care in lost appointments.

### DIY vs partner: when to refer to Buzprout

**DIY builders** (template sites clients update themselves) work when:

- 1 to 5 pages that rarely change
- No bookings, dashboards, or custom logic
- Budget under ~JMD $30,000/year all-in

**Refer to Buzprout** when:

- Bookings, payments, or admin tools are needed
- WhatsApp and spreadsheets are breaking under volume
- Delivery tracking, portals, or multi-user systems are required
- The client wants monthly payments instead of one large invoice

## Matching referrals to the right tier

Use this quick guide when a client comes through a referrer:

**"I just need to be online"** → Entry (Starter Website or Landing Page)

**"I need bookings / enquiries / the site to help run the business"** → Growth (Business Website + Systems)

**"I need tracking, dashboards, or software for my team"** → Systems (Custom Platform or Logistics Lite)

**"The site is live but nobody maintains it"** → Care Essential, Standard, or Plus

**"We have custom software and keep needing changes"** → Business Systems or Growth Retainer

**Always ask:** What happens if the site breaks before a busy weekend? Who updates prices when they change?

## Worked example: The Beauty Refinery JA (ongoing care)

Build for this client is **already agreed**. This section is about **what comes after** — monthly care for the live operating stack.

### Business profile

- Salon with **two locations**
- Appointments booked **through the website**
- **Deposits** depend on clear, correct instructions on the site
- Service menus and training prices **change regularly**
- **Admin dashboard** for staff (bookings, applications, insights, team roles)
- **Automated emails** after appointments and for monthly rebook reminders
- The stack is **operations**, not just marketing

### Recommended ongoing package

**Care (lean Essential package):** JMD $5,000/month**  
Sits within the published Care Essential band (JMD $4,000 to $6,000) but sized for a **booking- and ops-dependent salon**:

- Uptime and security monitoring for the public site **and** admin tools
- Booking-flow, application form, and email-path checks
- Reasonable monthly updates: prices, services, hours, policies, locations
- Light staff guidance for dashboard use (logins, roles, where applications live)
- Oversight of hosting, database, domain, and transactional email health
- A dedicated contact when something breaks

If the salon needs faster turnaround or heavier dashboard / email change volume, step up to **Care Standard (JMD $8,000 to $12,000/month)**.

### What they pay going forward (illustrative)

- **Care:** JMD $5,000/month  
- **Infrastructure:** domain, hosting, database, email sending — low at first; may grow with usage (client-owned or at cost)

**Year picture at $5,000/month:** about **JMD $60,000/year** for ownership of a live booking site, staff admin, applications, and client follow-up emails — far less than repeated emergency fixes plus lost appointments.

### Why the referrer can stand behind this

> Monthly care (JMD $5,000) keeps the live system working: public booking site, staff admin dashboard, application intake, and client email follow-ups. Platform hosting and email tools are separate and still need someone to manage them. At this rate, care is a light retainer, not agency overhead. Without it, every fix is a separate bill and bookings, staff tools, or emails can fail quietly.

## What clients pay separately (not in build or care)

Be clear that these are **additional** and help explain why "cheap hosting" does not mean "cheap everything":

- Domain registration and renewal
- Hosting or database plan upgrades as traffic and data grow
- Paid messaging or SMS credits
- Photography, copywriting, or branding beyond scope
- Advertising and SEO campaigns
- New major features (card payments, staff dashboards, SMS automation)
- Emergency after-hours work outside agreed care hours

---

## What is not included in standard care

To keep monthly fees fair and predictable:

- Full redesign or rebrand
- New major features scoped as separate projects
- Unlimited content changes (Care Standard/Plus add formal edit hours for higher volume)
- Third-party platform invoices, at cost or paid directly by the client
